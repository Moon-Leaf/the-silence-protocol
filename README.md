## 🧠 The Problem

We live in an age of infinite input and fractured attention.

The average person receives **60–80 notifications per day**, switches between apps every **3 minutes**, and consumes more information in a week than a person in the 1800s did in a **lifetime**. The result is not productivity — it is **digital exhaustion**.

- 📱 Notification overload hijacks focus and raises cortisol levels
- 🔄 Infinite scroll and algorithmic feeds are engineered for compulsion, not value
- 💬 Always-on communication culture erodes psychological safety and deep work
- 🧩 Context switching fragments creative and cognitive capacity
- 😶 We have optimized for *connection* while losing *presence*

**The Silence Protocol** is a response to this crisis — a structured, open, and human-centered framework for building **Digital Peace of Mind**.

---

## 🎯 What Is The Silence Protocol?

The Silence Protocol is an **open-source framework** of communication rules, technology boundaries, and intentional design principles that help individuals, teams, and communities reduce digital noise and recover cognitive sovereignty.

It is not about disconnecting from technology. It is about **reconnecting with yourself** while using technology — on your own terms.

> Think of it as a social contract between you and the digital world.

---

## ✨ Core Principles

### 1. 🔕 Silence by Default
Notifications, pings, and alerts should require **opt-in**, not opt-out. Silence is the healthy baseline; noise must earn its place. Every interruption costs attention — make it justify its cost.

### 2. ⏳ Asynchronous First
Not every message needs an immediate reply. The Protocol establishes **response windows** — defined periods for communication — so that focus is protected and urgency is never manufactured. Deep work is a protected resource.

### 3. 🧱 Intentional Boundaries
Technology use should be deliberate. The Protocol provides **boundary templates** for individuals and teams: device-free zones, focus blocks, notification schedules, and digital curfews that align with human energy cycles.

### 4. 🌿 Minimal Interface Design
Interfaces, workflows, and digital environments should follow a **low-stimulation design** philosophy — removing dark patterns, reducing choice paralysis, and surfacing only what is essential. Less friction toward depth, more friction toward distraction.

### 5. 🤝 Compassionate Communication Norms
Silence should never be weaponized. The Protocol builds a **shared vocabulary of digital etiquette** — clear signals for availability, honest acknowledgment of response delays, and mutual respect for each other's cognitive space.

## 🚀 Getting Started

You don't need to install anything. You need to **decide something**.

### For Individuals

1. **Read the Personal Protocol** → [`/docs/personal-protocol.md`](docs/personal-protocol.md)
2. Choose your starting tier:
   - 🌱 **Seed** — One small change (e.g., phone-free mornings)
   - 🌿 **Sprout** — A weekly silence schedule
   - 🌳 **Root** — Full intentional digital lifestyle design
3. Track your experience in [`/journal/`](journal/) using the provided templates
4. Share your reflection in [Discussions](../../discussions)

### For Teams

1. **Read the Team Protocol** → [`/docs/team-protocol.md`](docs/team-protocol.md)
2. Run the **Noise Audit** workshop → [`/workshops/noise-audit.md`](workshops/noise-audit.md)
3. Adopt a **Communication Contract** → [`/templates/communication-contract.md`](templates/communication-contract.md)
4. Schedule a monthly **Protocol Review** using our retro template

### For Developers & Builders

1. Fork this repository
2. Browse [`/tools/`](tools/) for scripts and configs (focus timers, notification blockers, shell tools)
3. Read the **Technical Specification** → [`/docs/technical-spec.md`](docs/technical-spec.md)
4. Check [`/research/`](research/) for evidence-based references and case studies
5. See [Contributing](#-contributing) to add your own tools or improve the framework
