# Team Protocol – A Guide for Teams & Organizations

This protocol is most powerful when adopted collectively. Here's how to bring it to your team.

## Step 1: Understand Your Team's Cognitive Load
Run the [Noise Audit](../workshops/noise-audit.md) (60 minutes). Map out: how many notifications does each member receive daily? How many are truly important?

## Step 2: Agree on a Communication Contract
Use the [Communication Contract template](../templates/communication-contract.md). Fill it out together:
- Primary Response Windows.
- Emergency protocol (passphrase, dedicated channel).
- Rules for communication outside work hours.

## Step 3: Adopt Gradually
- Week 1–2: One principle only, e.g., “Response Windows”.
- Week 3–4: Add “Async-First” as the default.
- Month 2: Evaluate with a team retrospective (a `quarterly-review` workshop guide will be available in `workshops/`).

## Step 4: Respect Each Other's Time
- Include your Response Window in your email signature.
- Treat `deep_focus` status as “working deeply”, not “away”.
- Schedule messages to be sent during the recipient's work hours.

## The Role of Managers
- Lead by example: don't send messages outside work hours.
- Evaluate based on outcomes, not response speed.
- Protect the team's right to focus.

> *“A quiet team is a productive team.”*
